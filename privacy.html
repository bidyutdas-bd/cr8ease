Privacy Policy

Privacy Notice

As part of the EU’s General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) ‘Personal Data’ is defined as:

“any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (‘data subject’); an identifiable natural person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as a name, an identification number, location data, an online identifier or to one or more factors specific to the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural or social identity of that natural person”.

Voktis Creative is legally obliged to respect your privacy and is committed to protecting your Personal Data. This Privacy Notice will inform you as to how we look after your Personal Data, tell you about your privacy rights and how the law protects you.

Purpose of this Privacy Notice

This Privacy Notice aims to give you information on how Voktis Creative collects and processes your Personal Data through our interactions with you.

We will comply with the GDPR. This says that Personal Data we hold about you must be:

  • Used lawfully, fairly and in a transparent way
  • Collected only for valid purposes that we have clearly explained to you and not used in any way that is incompatible with those purposes
  • Relevant to the purposes we have told you about and limited only to those purposes
  • Accurate and kept up to date
  • Kept only as long as necessary for the purposes we have told you about
  • Kept securely

It is important that you read this Privacy Notice so that you are fully aware of how and why we are using your data.

Changes to the Privacy Notice and your duty to inform us of changes

To ensure we follow this notice, it is essential that the Personal Data we hold about you is accurate and current. It is your responsibility to keep us informed if your Personal Data changes during your relationship with us.

The data we collect about you

  • Identity Data: first name, maiden name, last name, username, marital status, title, date of birth, gender.
  • Contact Data: billing & delivery addresses, email, phone numbers.
  • Financial Data: bank account, payment card details, payroll data.
  • Transaction Data: payments, products, services purchased.
  • Technical Data: IP address, login data, browser type/version, timezone, OS, platform.
  • Profile Data: website/intranet username, purchases, preferences, feedback/surveys.
  • Usage Data: how you use our website, intranet, products/services.
  • Marketing & Communications Data: preferences for receiving marketing.

We also collect aggregated data for statistical purposes. Aggregated Data may be derived from your Personal Data but is not considered Personal Data in law.

If you are unable to provide Personal Data

Where Personal Data is needed by law or contract and you fail to provide it, we may be unable to perform the contract and may have to cancel products/services, but we will notify you.

Data collection methods

We collect data through direct interactions, website forms, emails, phone calls, cookies, analytics, and third-party providers such as Google and data brokers.

How we use your Personal Data

Personal Data is used only when legally allowed. Common uses include performing contracts, legitimate interests, compliance with law, protecting interests, and marketing communications (with consent).

Marketing & Cookies

Marketing communications are sent only with consent. Cookies are used for analytics, social media, forms, newsletters, and improving the website experience. Disabling cookies may break some features.

Change of purpose & International transfers

Personal Data is used only for the purposes collected, unless compatible. Transfers outside the EEA follow legal safeguards.

Data security & retention

Personal Data is secured, access is limited, and breaches are reported as required. Data is kept only as long as necessary; some may be anonymised indefinitely.
